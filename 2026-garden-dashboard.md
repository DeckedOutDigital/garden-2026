### 🌤️ Shade Sections — Side Yard (Drip Zone 1, ~1 hr midday sun)

Each section is a right triangle ~5' × 3' ≈ 7.5 sq ft (×4 = 30 sq ft total)

| Section | Plants | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Shade 1 | Lettuce mix + radishes | Successive sow every 2–3 wks, spring through fall |
| Shade 2 | Kale (Lacinato or Red Russian) + Swiss chard | Cut-and-come-again all season |
| Shade 3 | Sorrel + parsley + cilantro | Sorrel is a shade-loving perennial with lemony flavor |
| Shade 4 | Spinach + arugula + mesclun mix | Bolt-resistant varieties; great for salad greens |

> **Shade strategy:** These sections are ideal for greens that bolt in full sun. The limited light actually extends your leafy green harvest well into summer when sun beds are too hot for lettuce.

### 🪴 Containers (2 × ~2.5' diameter round, 2' deep)

| Container | Plants | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Container 1 | 1 patio/determinate tomato (Patio Princess or Bushsteak) + parsley ring + marigolds | Needs full sun placement |
| Container 2 | 1 hot pepper (habanero or serrano) + trailing nasturtiums (edible flowers) | Full sun; nasturtiums attract pollinators |

> **Container tips:** At 2.5' diameter, these fit one main plant each with a small companion. Use quality potting mix (NOT garden soil). Containers dry faster than beds — check moisture even with drip lines. Add a tomato cage to Container 1.

## 📅 Monthly Planting Schedule

### Key: 🌱 = Direct Sow Seed | 🪴 = Buy & Plant Seedling | ✂️ = Harvest | 🧹 = Maintenance

| Month | Action | Details |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **MARCH** | 🧹 Plan & order | Order seeds online (cheaper than nursery). Buy: lettuce mix, spinach, pea, bean, zucchini, cucumber, radish, kale, chard, arugula, green onion, cilantro, nasturtium seeds. Reserve seedling purchases for May. |
| **APRIL (early)** | 🧹 Soil prep | Amend all beds with 2–3" compost. Turn lightly. Check drip lines for clogs. |
| **APRIL 1–15** | 🌱 Cool-season sow #1 | **Beds:** Peas (Bed B trellis), spinach (Bed C + Shade 4), lettuce (Bed C + Shade 1), radishes (Shade 1) |
| **APRIL 15–30** | 🌱 Cool-season sow #2 | **Shade beds:** Kale, chard (Shade 2), arugula/mesclun (Shade 4), cilantro (Shade 3), parsley (Shade 3) |
| **MAY 1–10** | 🌱 Last cool sows | Succession lettuce, cilantro, green onions (Bed C). Direct sow marigolds in all beds (after last frost). |
| **MAY 10–20** | 🪴 **Transplant day!** | Buy seedlings at local nursery: **tomatoes** (3 for Bed A, 1 patio for Container 1), **peppers** (4 for Bed A, 1 for Container 2), **parsley** (4–6 starts), **oregano, thyme, chives** (1 each for Bed C). Buy or sow **sorrel** (Shade 3). |
| **MAY 15–25** | 🌱 Warm-season sow | Direct sow **zucchini** (Bed B), **cucumbers** (Bed B), **bush beans** (Bed B), **nasturtiums** (Container 2). |
| **JUNE** | 🧹 Maintain | Mulch beds 2–3" (straw or wood chips). Train cucumbers up trellis. Succession sow lettuce in shade beds. Thin seedlings. Fertilize tomatoes/peppers (balanced 10-10-10 or fish emulsion). |
| **JUNE 15** | 🌱 Succession sow | Beans round 2, cilantro, lettuce (shade only — too hot in sun). |
| **JULY** | ✂️🧹 Peak season | Harvest peas (before heat kills them), lettuce, herbs, zucchini starts producing. Remove spent peas, let cucumbers take trellis. Deep water in heat waves. |
| **JULY 15** | 🌱 Fall prep sow | Start fall brassicas: kale, chard in shade beds (succession). Beans round 3. |
| **AUGUST** | ✂️ Heavy harvest | Tomatoes, peppers, zucchini, cucumbers, beans all producing. Keep harvesting parsley and herbs. Save tomato seeds if desired. |
| **AUG 15–SEPT 1** | 🌱 Fall/winter sow | Direct sow: spinach, lettuce, radishes, arugula in ALL beds for fall crop. Garlic cloves can go in late Sept/Oct for next year. |
| **SEPTEMBER** | ✂️ Continued harvest | Tomatoes/peppers until frost. Fall greens coming in. Watch overnight temps — cover tender plants if frost threatens. |
| **OCTOBER** | ✂️🧹 Frost prep | Harvest remaining tomatoes/peppers before hard frost (~Oct 5). Green tomatoes can ripen indoors. Pull spent plants. Leave kale/chard (frost improves flavor). Plant garlic cloves 4" deep for 2027. |
| **NOVEMBER** | 🧹 Winterize | Top beds with 3–4" compost/leaf mulch. Turn off drip lines. Clean and store cages/trellises. Collect/save marigold seed heads for 2027. |

## ✅ Master Checklist

### 🟡 Pre-Season (March–Early April)
- [ ] Order seed packets online (or buy at local nursery)
- [ ] Inventory supplies: compost, mulch, tomato cages, trellis materials
- [ ] Test drip irrigation — run each zone, check for leaks/clogs
- [ ] Clean beds of winter debris
- [ ] Amend all beds with 2–3" quality compost, turn lightly
- [ ] Amend shade sections with compost (may need to build up soil)
- [ ] Install trellis in Bed B (east half) for peas/cucumbers
- [ ] Prep containers with fresh potting mix (not reused soil)

### 🟢 Planting Phase (April–May)
- [ ] **Apr 1–15:** Direct sow peas, spinach, lettuce, radishes (cool crops)
- [ ] **Apr 15–30:** Sow kale, chard, arugula, cilantro, parsley in shade beds
- [ ] **May 1–10:** Succession sow lettuce/cilantro; sow marigolds after last frost
- [ ] **May 10–20:** Buy and plant all seedlings (tomatoes, peppers, parsley, herbs)
- [ ] **May 15–25:** Direct sow zucchini, cucumbers, beans, nasturtiums
- [ ] Mulch all beds 2–3" after soil warms
- [ ] Set up tomato cages/supports at planting time (not later!)

### 🔵 Growing Season (June–August)
- [ ] Check drip irrigation weekly — adjust for heat
- [ ] Fertilize tomatoes/peppers every 2–3 weeks once fruiting
- [ ] Harvest parsley regularly to encourage new growth
- [ ] Train cucumbers up trellis weekly
- [ ] Succession sow lettuce in shade beds every 2–3 weeks
- [ ] Succession sow beans every 3–4 weeks
- [ ] Succession sow cilantro every 3 weeks
- [ ] Watch for common CO pests: aphids, squash bugs, tomato hornworms
- [ ] Hand-pick hornworms; use insecticidal soap for aphids if needed
- [ ] Remove spent pea vines when they decline (July)
- [ ] Harvest zucchini SMALL (6–8") — they grow fast!

### 🟠 Fall & Harvest (September–October)
- [ ] **Aug 15–Sept 1:** Sow fall spinach, lettuce, radishes, arugula
- [ ] Monitor frost forecasts starting late September
- [ ] Cover tender plants (tomatoes, peppers) on frost nights
- [ ] Harvest all tomatoes/peppers before hard freeze
- [ ] Ripen green tomatoes indoors (paper bag with banana)
- [ ] Leave kale and chard — they taste better after frost
- [ ] **Late October:** Plant garlic cloves for 2027 harvest
- [ ] Save marigold seed heads for next year (dry and store)

### ⚪ Winterize (November)
- [ ] Pull all spent annual plants
- [ ] Top dress beds with 3–4" compost or leaf mulch
- [ ] Shut off and drain drip irrigation lines
- [ ] Clean and store tomato cages, trellises, tools
- [ ] Note what worked and what didn't for 2027 planning

## 💧 Irrigation Notes

- **All beds and shade sections share one drip zone** — this means watering schedule must balance the thirstiest plants
- **Summer target:** Run drip 20–30 min daily in peak heat (July–Aug), every other day in spring/fall
- **Containers:** Check daily — they dry out faster than beds. Consider hand-watering containers as a supplement
- **Mulch is critical:** 2–3" straw/wood chip mulch reduces water needs by 30–50%
- **Morning watering preferred** to reduce disease pressure
